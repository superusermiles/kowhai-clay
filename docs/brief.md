# Kōwhai & Clay — Project Brief

## Business Overview
Kōwhai & Clay is an artisan pottery studio and café in Raglan, Waikato. The business blends handmade ceramics, small-group wheel and hand-building workshops, an open studio membership offering, and a relaxed coastal café serving espresso, cabinet bakes, and seasonal light meals. The brand sits between creative retreat, local gathering place, and design-led retail.

**Tagline:** Shaped by the sea.

## Brand Positioning
Kōwhai & Clay is not a generic pottery class venue. It is a warm, tactile, coastal studio with an intentional point of view: slow craft, natural materials, imperfect beauty, and connection to place. The experience should feel calm, sun-washed, handmade, and quietly premium.

## Goals
1. Introduce the business clearly and memorably.
2. Drive workshop bookings.
3. Explain the open studio offering for returning makers.
4. Showcase the café as a destination, not an afterthought.
5. Build trust through story, photography, and process.
6. Make visiting simple with hours, map, FAQs, and contact.

## Target Audience
- Raglan locals looking for a creative third place.
- Weekend visitors from Waikato and Auckland seeking an experience.
- Beginners interested in pottery workshops.
- Intermediate makers wanting open studio access.
- Design-conscious café customers drawn to handmade ceramics and coastal atmosphere.
- Gift buyers looking for classes, ceramics, or memorable experiences.

## Brand Voice
- Warm, grounded, and welcoming.
- Thoughtful, sensory, and place-led.
- Unhurried, never corporate.
- Clear and practical when explaining bookings, class structure, and visiting details.

## Conversion Priorities
- Primary: Book a workshop.
- Secondary: Visit the café, enquire about open studio, contact the team.
- Tertiary: Follow on Instagram, browse gallery, understand the studio story.

## Content Direction
Content should emphasize texture, process, sea air, kiln warmth, clay-covered hands, shelves of glazed pieces, sunlight on timber, and plated café dishes served in handmade ceramics. Copy should be concise, poetic in small doses, and useful when details matter.

## Visual Direction
The site should feel handmade and coastal, with earthy warmth balanced by fresh ocean air. Use asymmetry, generous white space, tactile photography, and subtle organic shapes. Avoid polished corporate layouts, sharp tech gradients, or generic café tropes.
